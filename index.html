How to Use AquaVision

  1. 1

    Choose the Organism

    Select L. vannamei, P. monodon, M. salmoides, or M. rosenbergii before you upload. Each species carries its own segmentation model and allometric constants, so the selection must match the animal you are measuring.

  2. 2

    Place the Specimen

    Lay specimens flat and separated, on a plain non-reflective surface. Shoot top-down under even, glare-free light, and use the calibrated eight-inch tray for M. salmoides so the pixel scale stays true.

  3. 3

    Initiate the Analysis

    Upload a single frame or a whole batch, then run the analysis. Hold camera height, lens, and zoom constant across the set — changing any of them mid-batch invalidates the calibration.

  4. 4

    Read the Instrument

    Weigh mean length and mass against your harvest thresholds, and read the coefficient of variation as the uniformity index for the cohort. Re-shoot any frame where specimens overlap, touch, or fall outside the tray.

About the Lab

Precision Science for Aquaculture Communities

At UAPB Fish Nutrigenomics & AI Lab, we believe in the transformative power of precision science and the boundless potential it unlocks for aquaculture communities across Arkansas and beyond.

Our mission is to foster research that empowers farmers, drives food security, and advances the science of aquatic species detection through deep learning and computer vision.
